ALTO XINGU REGION

Kuarup

The Kuarup, also known as Quarup, is an important ceremonial ritual performed by various indigenous ethnicities in Brazil, especially those inhabiting the Alto Xingu region, such as the Kamayurá, Kalapalo, Kuikuro, Waurá, among others. This ritual is considered one of the most significant and complex celebrations within these communities.

The Kuarup ceremony is conducted to honor individuals of importance who have passed away, often leaders, valiant warriors, or other notable figures in the community. The objective of the ceremony is to pay tribute to the deceased, celebrate their lives, and facilitate the transition of their spirits to the spiritual realm.

Preparation for the Kuarup ritual involves meticulous planning and can span several months. It includes the collection of natural materials like straw, feathers, wood, and pigments to craft ornaments, masks, and decorations. Each element used in the ceremony holds specific symbolic meanings.
