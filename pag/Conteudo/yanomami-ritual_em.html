YANOMAMI

Reahu

The main Yanomami ritual is the ceremony of the dead, called reahu.

To contact the spirits of their loved ones, members of the tribe inhale a hallucinogenic powder called paric√°. In the body, the component extracted from tree bark leaves indigenous people in a state of trance. In this way, they believe, they open space to incorporate the spirits of those who have passed away.

The Yanomami funeral ritual is also unique. Whenever a member of the ethnic group dies, their body is cremated and their ashes are incorporated into banana porridge. The mixture is distributed among the members of the village. The belief is that the spirit of the deceased will protect everyone.
