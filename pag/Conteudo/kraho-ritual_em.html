KRAHÔ

Rites

There are many Krahô rites. There are briefer ones, relating to individual crises (end of shelter due to the birth of the first child, end of convalescence, last meal of the deceased), or promoted by occasional collective initiatives, such as exchanges of food and services.

There are those related to the annual and agricultural cycle, such as those that mark the dry and rainy seasons, the planting and harvesting of corn, the harvest of sweet potatoes. There are those that are part of a cycle longer than the annual one, that of male initiation, which should follow each other in a certain order, which is difficult to reconstitute today, including due to the disappearance of one of them.

Several of the rites of the annual and initiatory cycles have myths that tell their origin. But there is no complete correspondence between the mythical and the ritual sequence, although, without a doubt, there are points of approximation.
