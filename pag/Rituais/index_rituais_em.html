Rites

Bororo

The Bororo Funeral Ritual (MT) marks a special moment of socialization of young people. Not only because many of them are formally started, but also because it is through their participation in the corners, Dances, hunts and collective fisheries that they have the opportunity to learn and perceive the richness of their culture.

Krahô

In the tree trunk race, which is related to the realization of different rituals, The Krahô (TO) are divided into two teams, said "halves". Each one of them carries a buriti trunk section (or other vegetable), whose format, size and ornamentation are variable

Enawenê-Nawê

No Yãkwa, performed ritual by Enawenê-Nawê (MT), the inhabitants of the village, Divided into clans, make a widespread exchange of food, singing and dances. Ritual, which lasts several months and has two distinct phases, It aims to fulfill the teachings of the underground Spirits, Yakairiti.

Karajá

The first initiation of the Karajá Boys (MT/TO) is around seven or eight years old. It consists of drilling the lower lip, which will receive a adornment. Perforation is done with the clavicle of a monkey, and is in the presence of parents.

Yanomami

In the Yanomami (AM) Maloca tootobi, men hold session with hallucinogenic powder, Yãkuãna. This is very present in the initiation of the Yanomami shamans, and should always be under the conduct of the elders.

Kadiwéu

The "Bobos" (bobotegi) are characters that appear at the Ship Festival, held by the Kadiwéu. This long ritual dates back to the times of the Paraguayan War, when these people fought for Brazil.
